1) What are some examples of "clients" you've used today?
Laptop, phone, watch

2) How would you explain what a "server" is to a 5 year-old?

Imagine a big toy box in your friend’s house that has all the toys you like.

When you visit, you ask, “Can I have the car toy?” and your friend takes it from the box and gives it to you.

The toy box is like the server: it keeps all the toys safe and gives the right toy when someone asks.

3) In what way do clients and servers interact with each other?
Imagine you (client) ask the toy‑box friend (server), “Please give me the red car.”

Your friend hears you, finds the red car, and hands it to you.

If you want another toy, you ask again, and the friend gives it; that asking and giving keeps repeating.

Technical summary
The client sends a request over the network saying what data or service it wants.

The server receives the request, processes it (maybe talks to a database), and sends a response back.

Your browser, app, or game client does this many times, and the server can handle requests from many clients at once.
